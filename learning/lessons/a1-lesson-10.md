## Grammar Point: Compound Words

In Voku, you create new words by combining existing roots. The **modifier** comes first, the **head** (main word) comes last:

```
modifier + head = compound

kela + teru = kela-teru    (information-system = database)
runa + alati = runa-alati    (agent-important = supervisor)
pera + tore = pera-tore    (error-search = debugging)
```

### Rules
1. Modifier before head
2. Hyphen between parts in romanization
3. The final vowel of the head determines the compound's category
4. Meaning is always transparent from the parts

### Category from Final Vowel

```
kela-teru  → ends in -u → abstraction (a system-concept)
runa-alati  → ends in -i → quality (an important-type of agent)
pera-tore  → ends in -e → action (the act of error-searching)
kela-novi  → ends in -i → quality (a new-type of information)
```

## A1 Grammar Review

### The Five Word Categories

| Final Vowel | Category | Examples |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| -a | Entity (noun) | runa, kela, toka, sola |
| -e | Action (verb) | take, sene, kele, meke |
| -i | Quality (adjective) | novi, veri, tomi, rapi |
| -o | Relation (preposition) | eno, kono, poro, eso |
| -u | Abstraction (meta) | voku, teru |

### The Three Core Modes

| Mode | Particle | Use |
|------|----------|-----|
| Declarative | ka | State facts |
| Interrogative | ve | Ask questions |
| Imperative | to | Give commands |

### The Three Tenses

| Tense | Prefix | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Past | te- | te-take (did) |
| Present | zero/nu- | take (do) |
| Future | fu- | fu-take (will do) |

### Negation and Confirmation

| Word | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| mu | no, not |
| su | I confirm, yes |

---

## Examples

1. **Ka sol te-tore kela eno kela-teru.**
   - DECL 1SG PST-search information in database
   - I searched for information in the database.

2. **Ve nor pote take pera-tore?**
   - Q 2SG can do error-search
   - Can you do debugging?

3. **To nor voke kono runa-alati.**
   - IMP 2SG communicate with supervisor
   - Communicate with the supervisor.

4. **Ka kela-novi vali poro solri-kon.**
   - DECL information-new good for 1PL.INCL
   - The update is good for all of us.

5. **Ka sol fu-lare eno loka-lare.**
   - DECL 1SG FUT-learn in school
   - I will learn at school.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Build compounds
Combine these words into compounds and translate:
1. mesa + novi = ___ → ___
2. tama + tomi = ___ → ___
3. runa + hele = ___ → ___

**Answers:** 1. mesa-novi → new message / news, 2. tama-tomi → big house / mansion, 3. runa-hele → helper / assistant

### Exercise 2: Comprehensive translation (Voku to English)
1. Ka sol te-sene sola eno hima.
2. Ve nor fu-take toka kono sol?
3. To norri lare voku eno loka-lare.
4. Ka vel te-meke kela-novi poro solri-kon.
5. Ka sol mu-kele kela de-nor.

**Answers:**
1. I saw the sun in the sky.
2. Will you do the task with me?
3. (All of you) learn Voku at school.
4. He created an update for all of us.
5. I don't know your information.

### Exercise 3: Comprehensive translation (English to Voku)
1. She will search for the database.
2. Do they know the supervisor?
3. Open the house and come in.
4. I ate fruit and bread.
5. The water is cold and good.

**Answers:**
1. Ka vel fu-tore kela-teru.
2. Ve velri kele runa-alati?
3. To nor aze tama en vine.
4. Ka sol te-ane fura en pana.
5. Ka ama koli en vali.

**Answers:**

### Exercise 5: Write a dialogue
Write a 6-line dialogue between two agents. Use at least all three modes (ka, ve, to) and two tenses.

**Sample answer:**
```
A: Alo. Ve nor te-fine toka? Finu.
   (Hello. Did you finish the task? Your turn.)

B: Mu. Sol mu-te-fine toka. Sol neke hele. Finu.
   (No. I didn't finish the task. I need help. Your turn.)

A: Su. To nor voke kela de-toka poro sol. Finu.
   (Confirmed. Tell me about the task. Your turn.)

B: Ka pera eno kela-teru. Sol mu-pote mute. Finu.
   (There is an error in the database. I can't change it. Your turn.)

A: Ka sol fu-hele nor. To nor aze kela-teru. Finu.
   (I will help you. Open the database. Your turn.)

B: Ka vali. Ka sol-sati. Klosu.
   (Good. I am satisfied. Goodbye.)
```
