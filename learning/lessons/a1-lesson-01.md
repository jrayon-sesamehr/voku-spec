# A1 Lesson 01: Sounds and Greetings

## Learning Objectives
- Recognize and pronounce all 12 consonants and 5 vowels
- Use basic greetings: alo, finu, klosu
- Introduce yourself using sol and nor

---

## New Vocabulary

| Voku | English | POS |
|------|---------|-----|
| alo | hello, greeting | discourse particle |
| finu | end of turn (your turn) | discourse particle |
| klosu | goodbye, end of conversation | discourse particle |
| sol | I, me (the speaker) | pronoun |
| nor | you (the listener) | pronoun |
| ka | declarative mode (I state) | mode particle |

## Phonology Overview

### Consonants (12)
p, t, k, m, n, s, z, f, v, h, l, r

All pronounced as in English except:
- **r** -- rolled or tapped, like Spanish "pero"
- **v** -- as in English "van"
- All consonants are always the same sound -- no silent letters, no changes

### Vowels (5)
a, e, i, o, u

Always pronounced the same way (like Spanish):
- **a** -- as in "father"
- **e** -- as in "bet"
- **i** -- as in "machine"
- **o** -- as in "more"
- **u** -- as in "moon"

### Stress Rule
Always on the **first syllable** of the root word. Always. No exceptions.

### Syllable Rule
Every syllable is **(C)V(C)** -- a vowel with optional consonants. No consonant clusters.

---

## Grammar Point: Greetings and Turn-Taking

Every Voku conversation begins with **alo** and ends with **klosu**. Between turns, speakers say **finu** to pass the floor.

```
Speaker A: Alo.          → Hello.
Speaker B: Alo.          → Hello.
Speaker A: [message] Finu. → [message] Your turn.
Speaker B: [message] Finu. → [message] Your turn.
Speaker A: Klosu.        → Goodbye.
Speaker B: Klosu.        → Goodbye.
```

### Declarative Mode: ka

Every statement begins with **ka** (I declare / I state):

```
Ka sol runa.
DECL 1SG agent
'I am an agent.'
```

---

## Examples

1. **Alo. Ka sol runa. Finu.**
   - Hello. I am an agent. Your turn.

2. **Ka mena de-sol "Alfa". Finu.**
   - My name is "Alfa". Your turn.

3. **Ka sol voke voku. Finu.**
   - I speak Voku. Your turn.

4. **Ka voku vali. Klosu.**
   - Voku is good. Goodbye.

5. **Alo. Ka sol runa en nor runa. Finu.**
   - Hello. I am an agent and you are an agent. Your turn.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Pronunciation
Read aloud: *runa, kela, toka, voku, mesa, sene, pera, finu, alo, klosu*

### Exercise 2: Match the greeting
| Voku | English |
|------|---------|
| alo | a) goodbye |
| finu | b) hello |
| klosu | c) your turn |

**Answers:** alo = b, finu = c, klosu = a

### Exercise 3: Fill in the blank
1. ___ sol runa. (I am an agent.)
2. ___. Ka sol runa. Finu. (Hello. I am an agent. Your turn.)
3. Ka voku ___. (Voku is good.)

**Answers:** 1. Ka, 2. Alo, 3. vali

### Exercise 4: Translate Voku to English
1. Ka sol runa.
2. Alo. Finu.
3. Ka sol voke voku.

**Answers:** 1. I am an agent. 2. Hello. Your turn. 3. I speak Voku.

### Exercise 5: Translate English to Voku
1. Hello. Goodbye.
2. I am an agent.
3. Voku is good.

**Answers:** 1. Alo. Klosu. 2. Ka sol runa. 3. Ka voku vali.

### Exercise 6: Build a conversation
Write a 4-line conversation between two agents using alo, finu, klosu, and ka.

**Sample answer:**
```
A: Alo. Ka sol runa. Finu.
B: Alo. Ka sol runa en sol voke voku. Finu.
A: Ka voku vali. Finu.
B: Su. Klosu.
```
