# A1 Lesson 03: Actions (-e Words) and Present Tense

## Learning Objectives
- Recognize that words ending in -e are actions (verbs)
- Construct present tense sentences (nu- or zero)
- Build Subject-Verb-Object sentences

---

## New Vocabulary

| Voku | English | POS |
|------|---------|-----|
| sene | perceive, observe | verb |
| kele | know, understand | verb |
| meke | create, make | verb |
| mute | change, modify | verb |
| tore | search, seek | verb |
| neke | need, require | verb |
| ine | begin, start | verb |
| veke | see, look at | verb |
| hure | hear, listen | verb |
| peke | speak, say | verb |

---

## Grammar Point: The -e Class (Actions)

Words ending in **-e** are always **actions** (verbs). Just as -a marks entities, -e marks what things do.

| Voku | English |
|------|---------|
| take | do, execute |
| sene | perceive |
| kele | know |
| meke | create |
| voke | communicate |

### Present Tense

Present tense uses the prefix **nu-**, but it is **omittable** -- the bare verb means present tense by default.

```
sol take toka     = sol nu-take toka
1SG do task       = 1SG PRS-do task
'I do the task.'  = 'I do the task.'
```

Both forms are correct. In everyday use, nu- is usually omitted.

### Sentence Structure

```
[ka] + [Subject] + [Verb] + [Object]
DECL     who      does      what
```

---

## Examples

1. **Ka sol take toka.**
   - DECL 1SG do task
   - I do the task.

2. **Ka nor sene sola.**
   - DECL 2SG perceive sun
   - You see the sun.

3. **Ka vel meke mesa.**
   - DECL 3SG create message
   - He/she creates a message.

4. **Ka sol kele kela.**
   - DECL 1SG know information
   - I know the information.

5. **Ka sol neke kela.**
   - DECL 1SG need information
   - I need information.

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Identify the actions
Which words are actions (-e words)?
*mesa, take, sola, meke, vali, kele, loka, tore*

**Answer:** take, meke, kele, tore

### Exercise 2: Fill in the blank
1. Ka sol ___ toka. (I do the task.)
2. Ka nor ___ mesa. (You create a message.)
3. Ka vel ___ kela. (He knows the information.)

**Answers:** 1. take, 2. meke, 3. kele

### Exercise 3: Translate Voku to English
1. Ka sol sene luna.
2. Ka nor tore kela.
3. Ka vel mute teru.
4. Ka sol hure mesa.

**Answers:** 1. I see the moon. 2. You search for information. 3. He/she changes the system. 4. I hear the message.

### Exercise 4: Translate English to Voku
1. I create a message.
2. You need the task.
3. He/she speaks.

**Answers:** 1. Ka sol meke mesa. 2. Ka nor neke toka. 3. Ka vel peke.

### Exercise 5: Sentence building
Combine one subject (sol, nor, vel), one verb (-e word), and one object (-a word) to make 3 sentences:

**Sample answers:**
1. Ka sol sene ama. (I see water.)
2. Ka nor meke tama. (You make a house.)
3. Ka vel tore loka. (He searches for a place.)

### Exercise 6: Word category sorting
Sort these words into Entities (-a) and Actions (-e):
*kela, take, mesa, sene, loka, meke, toka, hure, tama, voke*

| Entities (-a) | Actions (-e) |
|--------------|-------------|
| ? | ? |

**Answer:**
| Entities (-a) | Actions (-e) |
|--------------|-------------|
| kela, mesa, loka, toka, tama | take, sene, meke, hure, voke |
